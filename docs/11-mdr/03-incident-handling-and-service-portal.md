# Incident Detection and Handling

When suspicious activity is detected by EDR or XDR, MDR analysts investigate and determine whether it represents a true security incident.

## Notifications and Escalation

Once an incident is confirmed, the escalation contacts receive an email notification with a link to the MDR Service Portal. Critical incidents may trigger a phone escalation.

## Response and Mitigation Actions

Depending on the service level, severity, approvals, and licensing, MDR may isolate endpoints, stop malicious processes, quarantine files, or perform recovery actions.

## Threat Hunting

Threat hunting is available with Advanced MDR only. Advanced MDR includes monthly proactive threat hunting activities designed to identify advanced or stealthy threats that may not trigger standard alerts.

# Reporting

## Standard MDR Reports

Standard MDR customers receive monthly operational reports covering deployment status, incidents, and detection statistics.

## Advanced MDR Reports

Advanced MDR customers receive executive summaries, detailed incident reports, root cause analysis, and threat hunting reports.

# Using the MDR Service Portal

Once an incident is confirmed, the escalation contacts receive an email notification with a link to the MDR Service Portal. The MDR Service Portal is the primary interface for interacting with MDR incidents.

## Viewing Resolved and Active Incidents

1. Log in to the Acronis MDR service portal (the MDR team has provided the link to the service portal by email).
2. On the welcome screen, click **View MDR incidents**.

The Requests page is displayed, showing all incidents with the following columns:
- Reference (ticket ID)
- Summary (including host name, severity, positivity level)
- Status (e.g., L1/NEW, L2/INVESTIGATION, PENDING - WAITING)
- Created date
- Updated date
- Priority (e.g., Medium, High)

You can filter incidents by status, request type, customer, and more. To control the columns that appear in the list, click **Edit list view**.

## Reviewing Analyst Findings and Actions

1. In the list of incidents, click the incident that you want to review.
2. Check the incident details. The incident view shows:
   - Summary details (host name, severity, positivity level)
   - Status (e.g., L2/INVESTIGATION)
   - Activity log showing automatic responses and status changes
   - Request type (MDR Incident)
   - Shared contacts

## Adding Comments and Requesting Actions

In the Comments box of the incident details view, add a comment to explain your request. You can request additional investigation or request incident reopening through comments.

# Data Residency and Global Service Delivery for Acronis MDR

Acronis MDR is designed to deliver global, 24/7 threat detection and response while balancing data residency, scalability, and operational excellence.

## Architecture Overview

Acronis MDR uses a distributed architecture that separates endpoint telemetry, security investigations, and service operations. This approach enables:

- Regional data storage where applicable
- Efficient global threat hunting and correlation
- Continuous 24/7 service delivered by a follow-the-sun SOC model

## Where Your Data Is Stored

### Endpoint and Product Data

- Product and telemetry data generated by Acronis security products is stored in Acronis regional data centers, aligned with the customer's selected region.
- Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR) operational data is stored in three global processing regions: United States, Europe, and AsiaPacific (Singapore).

This model ensures performance, resilience, and regional availability while supporting advanced detection capabilities.

### Security Events Used for Threat Hunting

Security events used for proactive threat hunting are processed in a centralized analytics environment located in Europe. This centralized model enables cross-region correlation, faster detection of emerging threats, and consistent investigation quality across all customers.

Event data can be logically segmented to support customers operating in different geographies.

### MDR Investigations and Case Management

MDR investigation records, including analyst findings and customer communications, are stored in the Acronis' centralized case management system hosted in Europe. This includes:

- Incident investigation results
- Analyst notes and verdicts
- Customer communications related to MDR cases

Centralized case management ensures consistent service quality, auditability, and end-to-end traceability of security incidents.

## Global Service Delivery

Acronis MDR service operations follow a global delivery model. Security analysts from multiple regions work under a follow-the-sun SOC model, ensuring that monitoring and response are available 24/7 regardless of customer location.

All analysts operate under uniform processes, playbooks, and quality standards, ensuring consistent service delivery across all geographies.
