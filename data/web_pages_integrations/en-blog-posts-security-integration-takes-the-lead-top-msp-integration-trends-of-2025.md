As the threat landscape evolves
and client environments grow more complex, managed service providers (MSPs) are
rethinking how their technology stacks work together.
According to the new
Acronis MSP integration trends 2025 report
, this past year marked a decisive shift toward
security‑first,
automation‑centric and platform‑driven
operations. Integrations are no
longer a backend convenience — they’re a strategic imperative that directly
impacts service quality, margins and growth.
Whether you rely on native
platform capabilities or third‑party tools, one message is clear:
MSPs that
integrate strategically outperform those that simply add more tools
. And in
an era where MSPs manage 20+ tools on average, cohesive integration is the only
path to efficiency, scalability and profitability.
Here is a preview of the top
three integration trends shaping MSP operations today. For the full data
insights including charts, activation growth and strategic integration
priorities for 2026,
download the MSP integration trends 2025 report.

Trend 1: Security integration takes the lead
For the first time,
security
integrations are the fastest‑growing category among MSPs
, outpacing
traditional RMM and PSA connections by a wide margin. Threat escalation,
expanding compliance obligations and rising client expectations are driving
this shift.
The 2025 data shows
:
Security integration adoption more than
doubled (123%)
year over year.
XDR and MDR integrations saw record spikes, with third‑party XDR integrations growing
162% on average
.
The number of MSPs using Acronis’ native XDR capabilities grew by
136%
, reflecting a major shift toward unified security.
Button
Button
This reflects a broader industry
pivot from monitoring to protection. MSPs are consolidating their security
posture across backup, endpoint, email and identity, and leveraging
integrations that enable automated response, cross‑system correlation and
faster remediation.
Why it matters for 2026
Security is now the competitive differentiator.
MSPs that unify security with data protection and endpoint management will be
able to demonstrate measurable, outcome‑based value to clients.
Trend 2: Automation evolves beyond MSP‑specific tools
Automation is no longer limited
to RMM scripts or PSA workflows. In 2025, MSPs increasingly adopted
business‑wide
automation
, spanning operations, sales, finance and customer success — not
just IT service delivery.
A surprising finding from the
report:
General‑purpose automation tools like Zapier saw 146% activation
growth
, outpacing MSP‑specific automation platforms.
This tells us the modern MSP
wants
:
No‑code automation.
Cross‑department workflow orchestration.
Freedom from manual, repetitive tasks.
Automation that connects native and third‑party tools seamlessly.
Acronis’ native workflow
automation, launched in late 2025, also gained strong traction. With automation
built directly into the platform, MSPs can orchestrate workflows across backup,
disaster recovery, RMM, EDR / XDR and PSA tasks without managing APIs or
connectors.
Why it matters for 2026
The MSPs who grow fastest in 2026 will be those
who automate not just IT processes, but
their entire business
.

Button
Trend 3: The platform consolidation paradox
At first glance, 2025 revealed a
contradiction:
Adoption of
native platform capabilities
such as Acronis RMM and PSA skyrocketed (96% and 50% growth in
licenses, respectively).
Yet
third‑party integrations
also continued to grow significantly.
Button
Button
This isn’t a conflict; it’s a
strategy.
MSPs are embracing
platformization (MSP 3.0), consolidating core functions to reduce complexity,
while integrating specialized tools where they add targeted value.
MSPs choose integrated platforms
because they
:
Reduce tool sprawl.
Eliminate context switching.
Lower training and licensing overhead.
Improve response times and consistency.
At the same time, no MSP platform
can do everything, which is why maintaining a flexible, integration‑friendly
tech stack is essential. Acronis’ ecosystem of 300+ integrations gives MSPs the
ability to consolidate intelligently while maintaining the flexibility they
need.
Why it matters for 2026
The winning MSPs will consolidate where it
matters, integrate where it counts and continuously evaluate tools based on
business value.
