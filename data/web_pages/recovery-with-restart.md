# Recovery with restart

Managing the backup and recovery of workloads and files > Recovery > Recovery with restart
Recovery with restart

A restart is required when you recover the following:

An operating system

For example, when you recover an entire machine or the system volume of a machine.

Encrypted volumes

For example, when you recover BitLocker-encrypted or CheckPoint-encrypted volumes.

Backed-up encrypted volumes are recovered as non-encrypted.

A recovery environment is automatically prepared on the recovered machine. When the environment is ready, the machine restarts, and then the recovery environment opens. When the recovery completes, the operating system starts.

Preparing the WinRE recovery environment might take up to three minutes.

For more information about the available recovery environments, see Recovery environments.
