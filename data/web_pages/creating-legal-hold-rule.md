# Creating a legal hold rule

Managing the backup and recovery of workloads and files > Email archiving > Legal hold rules > Creating a legal hold rule
Creating a legal hold rule

You can create a legal hold rule from the email archive or from the Management > Archiving plans tab.

Legal hold rules that are created from the email archive can use only one search query and one email archive.

Legal hold rules that are created from the Management > Archiving plans tab can use multiple search queries and multiple email archives.

To create a legal hold rule

From the email archive
From Management > Archiving plans
Run a search query on the email archive, as described in Running a saved search query.

Above the list of matching emails, click Apply legal hold rule.

[If there are existing legal hold rules] In the Apply legal hold rule to search query window, click Create legal hold rule.

To change the legal hold rule name, click the pencil icon, specify a new name, and then click OK.

In Legal hold period, select an option.

[If you select Hold until date] Select a date.

Click Save.

Emails that match this legal hold rule will be protected against deletion until this date, inclusive.

In Encryption password for archives, specify the encryption password for the email archive, and then click Proceed.

Click Create.

As a result, a legal hold rule is created.

The emails that match any of the search queries in this legal hold rule will be protected against deletion until the legal hold period ends.

If an email matches more than one legal hold rule, the rule with the longest legal hold period is applied.
